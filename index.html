Weather Patterns & Climate Variability Across Australia

How rainfall, temperature, and seasons differ across Australia's major cities.

Where Does It Rain the Most?

Darwin in the Northern Territory receives the highest annual rainfall of any major Australian city - over 1,700mm per year - driven by its tropical wet season. In contrast, Adelaide and Canberra are the driest capitals, receiving under 620mm annually.

Which Cities Are the Hottest?

Australia's north runs significantly hotter than its south. Darwin averages above 27°C year-round, while Hobart in Tasmania barely reaches 13°C on average - a difference of over 15 degrees between the two extremes.

Temperature Through the Year

Unlike the Northern Hemisphere, Australia's summer peaks in January and winter bottoms out in July. Darwin shows remarkably stable temperatures year-round, while Melbourne is known for its dramatic swings between seasons.

Rainfall Through the Year

Darwin's rainfall pattern is extreme - nearly all of its annual rain falls between November and April, with virtually none in the dry season. Perth shows the opposite pattern, receiving most of its rain in winter (June-August).
